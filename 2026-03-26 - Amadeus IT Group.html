§ 2.1 — What PSS Actually Does

The Operating System of Global Aviation

PSS is not a product airlines choose to use. It is the system that makes an airline operational. Without PSS, no flight departs.
Reservations (PNR)
Creates and manages every passenger name record. Every booking — direct, OTA, GDS, corporate — flows through this system. The PNR is the single source of truth. Amadeus processes ~690M bookings/year.
Inventory & Revenue Mgmt
Controls seat availability and pricing in real-time. Decides which fare classes are open, how many seats remain, what price to display. This is where airlines make money. Millions of optimisation decisions per day.
Departure Control (DCS)
Manages check-in, boarding, gate assignment, passenger processing. Prints boarding passes, validates documents, reconciles manifests. If DCS goes down, no one boards. BA's 2017 IT failure: £80M+ in one weekend.
Ticketing & Settlement
Issues e-tickets, manages fare rules, interline settlement. A single itinerary can involve 4 airlines, 3 currencies, 12 fare components. Complexity is the moat — no startup can replicate interline certification.

Why These Four Systems Cannot Be Separated

Reservations, inventory, DCS, and ticketing interact in real-time, millions of times per day. A booking instantly updates inventory (one fewer seat). Inventory feeds revenue management (reprice remaining seats). DCS pulls from reservations at check-in. Ticketing validates against all three. Switching one without the others is technically impossible — data formats, APIs, and 20+ years of custom business logic are deeply intertwined. Switching ALL FOUR simultaneously means running dual systems for 18–36 months: double cost, double failure risk, double IT staff. No airline CEO will risk grounding their fleet to save <2% on IT costs.
